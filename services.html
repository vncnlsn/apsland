Core Services

These are the services at the center of what we do — where our experience is deepest and where the quality of execution matters most.

Seismic Permit Acquisition

Core Service

We manage the end-to-end process of acquiring landowner access permits for 2D and 3D seismic surveys — coordinating with surface owners, mineral rights holders, and government agencies. This means explaining the project clearly, negotiating access terms, documenting every agreement, and confirming that permits are in place before crews arrive on-site.

  • Surface and mineral owner identification and contact
  • Permit negotiation and execution
  • Agency permitting — BLM, state agencies, railroad, highway
  • Permit tracking and compliance documentation

Landowner Relations & Field Oversight

Core Service

We don't stop at the signed permit. APS agents are on-site during active field operations to verify that crews are following agreed access routes, respecting property boundaries, and honoring the specific conditions each landowner negotiated. When something goes wrong — and it does, on every long project — we're already there to handle it before anyone else knows about it.

  • Pre-entry landowner coordination
  • On-site field monitoring during survey operations
  • Damage identification and claim documentation
  • Post-project restoration verification
  • Settlement negotiation for access claims

Title Research & Courthouse Records

Core Service

We trace surface ownership and mineral rights for every parcel along a project route — working directly through county courthouses, tax records, and deed books. Accurate research at the front end prevents the access disputes that stop a project mid-survey. It also tells you, before any landowner contact, exactly who you're dealing with and where the complications are.

  • Surface owner identification and verification
  • Mineral rights ownership research
  • Deed and chain-of-title examination
  • Parcel mapping and land description verification
  • Title curative work for problematic parcels

How a Project Works

The sequence, start to finish.

Every project follows the same general path. Here's where APS is involved at each stage — and what that looks like in practice.

1

Project Scoping & Design Review

Before any landowner contact happens, we review the proposed area or corridor to identify land status, ownership complexity, and any parcels likely to require extra time. This shapes the permit schedule so the client isn't caught off guard by a difficult parcel late in the program.

Typical duration: 1–3 weeks depending on acreage and data availability.

2

Title Research & Ownership Verification

We work through county courthouse records to confirm who owns each surface parcel and, where applicable, who holds the mineral rights. This step also catches title defects — missing heirs, incomplete deeds, split ownership — before they become field problems.

Run in parallel with landowner contact wherever possible to compress the schedule.

3

Landowner Contact & Permit Negotiation

Agents make personal contact with each landowner — by phone and in person. We explain the project in plain terms, answer their questions, and negotiate access conditions. Most landowners have reasonable concerns that can be addressed directly. Those that can't are flagged early so the client can decide how to proceed.

Every contact attempt and conversation is documented and delivered to the client.

4

Agency & Regulatory Permitting

Most projects also require approval from state agencies, BLM, county road departments, railroads, or utilities. We identify the required permits early and manage the application process so agency timelines don't become surprises once private landowner access is in hand.

Agency timelines vary significantly by state and permit type — factored in from the start.

5

Field Operations & On-Site Oversight

Once crews are in the field, APS agents are on-site to confirm that access routes are being followed and the terms of each permit are being respected. If a fence gets damaged, a crop gets driven through, or a gate gets left open, we're already there to document it and address it — before the landowner calls anyone.

Field oversight is where most permit firms stop. It's also where most landowner problems start.

6

Restoration & Project Closeout

After field work is complete, we verify that each property has been restored to the agreed condition, document any remaining damage claims, and negotiate settlements. We don't consider a project closed until the landowner is satisfied and every outstanding item is resolved.

A clean closeout protects the client's ability to go back to the same area in the future.
