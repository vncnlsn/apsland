Service 01

Permit Acquisition

Getting permits isn't just paperwork — it's navigation. APS manages the full permitting lifecycle for energy, pipeline, transmission, and infrastructure development projects. We coordinate directly with federal, state, and local agencies, track permit status, and anticipate delays before they become your problem.

We've built relationships with agencies across 48 states. When you need someone who knows the local process and who to call — that's us.

  • Surface use and right-of-way permits
  • Federal land permits (BLM, USFS, Army Corps of Engineers)
  • State agency coordination and compliance tracking
  • Municipal and county permit management
  • Permit status reporting and documentation

Service 02

Courthouse Research

Accurate land data is the foundation of any successful energy or infrastructure project. APS conducts thorough ownership verification, title research, parcel investigation, and record retrieval through county and municipal offices across the United States.

Our researchers know how to navigate local courthouse systems — the quirks, the backlogs, the exceptions. We've done it in hundreds of counties. You get clean, verified data, documented properly, delivered on your timeline.

  • Title and ownership verification
  • Parcel boundary and chain of title research
  • Mineral rights and lease record research
  • Easement and encumbrance identification
  • Tax record and lien research

Service 03

Landowner Relations

This is where projects succeed or stall — and it's where APS has always stood apart. We've been knocking on landowners' doors and negotiating agreements for over four decades. We show up as neighbors, not lawyers. That approach builds the kind of cooperation no contract can force.

From initial contact through agreement execution, APS manages every interaction with landowners, tenants, and community stakeholders with professionalism, patience, and genuine respect for the people whose land your project crosses.

  • Initial landowner contact and communication
  • Damage agreement and right-of-entry negotiation
  • Stakeholder coordination and concern resolution
  • Field agent deployment and status tracking
  • Documentation and agreement execution
