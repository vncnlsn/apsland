Core services

What APS is known for.

These are the services that sit at the center of the business and carry the most weight on a project.

Seismic Permit Acquisition

Core service

End-to-end permit acquisition for 2D and 3D seismic programs, including landowner contact, access negotiation, document execution, and permit tracking before crews mobilize.

  • Surface and mineral owner identification
  • Permit negotiation and execution
  • Government and agency permitting support
  • Permit tracking and compliance documentation

Landowner Relations & Field Oversight

Core service

APS stays involved after the permit is signed. Field oversight keeps the work aligned with the agreement, documents issues early, and protects the relationship with the landowner.

  • Pre-entry landowner coordination
  • On-site field monitoring
  • Damage and claim documentation
  • Restoration verification
  • Settlement support for access claims

Title Research & Courthouse Records

Core service

Ownership verification, deed research, parcel mapping, and mineral rights tracing so the project starts with a clear map of who owns what and where the risk lives.

  • Surface owner verification
  • Mineral rights research
  • Chain-of-title review
  • Parcel and land description mapping
  • Curative work on problem parcels

How a project works

The sequence, start to finish.

The order matters. This is the path APS uses to keep the work organized and the closeout clean.

1

Scoping and design review

Review the route or acreage, identify ownership complexity, and flag parcels that will need extra attention early.

Keeps the schedule realistic from day one.

2

Title research and verification

Confirm surface ownership, mineral interests, and title issues before the field team begins contact.

The part that prevents a lot of field trouble later.

3

Landowner contact and negotiation

Make direct contact, explain the project plainly, answer questions, and document the terms of access.

No fluff. Just clear conversation and records.

4

Agency and regulatory permitting

Coordinate with the agencies and third parties the project requires so approvals do not become a late-stage bottleneck.

Built into the plan instead of tacked on afterward.

5

Field oversight

Stay on site while crews are active so access routes, permit terms, and property protections are followed in real time.

The part most firms are missing.

6

Restoration and closeout

Verify restoration, resolve claims, and close the project down cleanly so the relationship remains usable for the next job.

A clean closeout protects the next project.
