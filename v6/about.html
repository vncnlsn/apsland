About APS

Built to be clear
in the field.

APS was founded in 1980 to handle the land work that projects depend on but rarely get credit for: access, research, permitting, and field follow-through.

Where it started

APS came out of a simple problem: projects kept stalling because land work was being handled inconsistently. The permits were incomplete, the records were messy, or nobody stayed close enough to the field to keep the work aligned with the agreement.

The company was built to make that part of the project steadier. Since then, APS has stayed focused on landowner contact, title work, permit acquisition, and closeout support for energy and infrastructure teams.

How we work

APS keeps the team tight and the process direct. That means the people managing the project are close to the work, the field agents know the region, and the client is not left guessing when something changes.

We aim for clean documentation, clear conversations, and steady follow-through. That is what keeps the work moving when the project gets complicated.

What stays constant

Landowners deserve real answers. Clients need honest schedules. Field work needs oversight, not assumptions. Those three ideas are the backbone of the way APS operates.

That approach is not flashy, but it is dependable. And dependability is what matters when the work has to hold up after everyone leaves the meeting.

Operating principles

Three things that do not change.

The details of each project change. The standard does not.

I

Accuracy first

Good research at the front end saves time later. APS does not rush the part of the job that makes the rest possible.

II

Plain language

Landowners and clients get direct answers, not polished language that avoids the question.

III

Present through closeout

The job is not done when the permit is signed. APS stays engaged until the fieldwork is complete and the project is closed out cleanly.
