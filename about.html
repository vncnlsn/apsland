About APS

Four decades of showing up
and following through.

How we started

Allstate Permit Services was founded in 1980 at a time when the energy industry was expanding rapidly across the United States and the work of securing landowner access — the critical step that makes any survey or pipeline possible — was still largely improvised. There was no standard playbook. Projects stalled because agents didn't have the right legal background, or because landowners weren't given straight answers, or because nobody stayed on-site long enough to make sure the work matched the agreement.

APS was built to solve that specific problem. Not just to acquire permits, but to manage the relationship between energy companies and the landowners whose property they depend on — doing it carefully, doing it right, and doing it in a way that held up once the crews arrived.

The work is quieter than the projects it enables. You won't see the APS name on a pipeline or a seismic report. But the access agreements, the title research, the signed permits, and the field oversight that allow those projects to run smoothly — that's what we've been doing since 1980.

How we work

APS is a mid-sized firm. That's intentional. We're large enough to staff multi-state projects and handle the full scope of what a major seismic or infrastructure client needs — and small enough that the people managing your project are the same people you talk to when something comes up.

Our field agents are experienced contractors we've worked with for years. We don't throw unfamiliar faces at sensitive landowner situations. The agents who knock on doors have done it before, in similar regions, for similar project types. They know how to explain a vibroseis survey to a fourth-generation rancher who has every reason to say no.

We also stay present during fieldwork — something a lot of permit acquisition firms don't do. If a crew deviates from the agreed access route, or leaves a gate open, or creates damage they weren't authorized to create, we hear about it before the landowner calls your office. That's the part of our work that doesn't show up in a service list but matters most to our clients.

What we stand for

Landowners aren't obstacles. They're people who have a legal right to understand what's happening on their land, and a legitimate interest in being treated with respect. We've always operated on that principle, and it's why the same clients keep calling us back.

Honesty is the foundation of every agreement we negotiate. When a landowner asks what the crew is going to do, what they're going to leave behind, and what happens if something goes wrong — they deserve a real answer. We give one.
